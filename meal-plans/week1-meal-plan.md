# Week 1 Meal Plan: Getting Started with Healthy Eating

## Overview

This meal plan focuses on simple, affordable ingredients available at most grocery stores. It emphasizes whole grains, lean proteins, fruits, vegetables, and reduced processed foods.

---

## Shopping List

### Proteins

- Chicken thighs (family pack, 8-10 pieces)
- Eggs (1 dozen)
- Canned or dried beans (black beans, kidney beans)
- Peanut butter (natural, no added sugar)

### Grains

- Old-fashioned oats (bulk container)
- Brown rice (2-3 lb bag)
- Whole wheat bread
- Whole wheat tortillas

### Dairy

- Milk (1 gallon)
- Plain yogurt (32 oz)
- Shredded cheese (1 lb)

### Produce (Fresh)

- Apples (5-6)
- Bananas (bunch)
- Carrots (2 lb bag)
- Broccoli (2 heads)
- Lettuce or spinach
- Tomatoes (3-4)

### Produce (Frozen)

- Mixed berries (16 oz bag)
- Mixed vegetables (2 lb bag)

### Pantry Staples

- Olive oil or vegetable oil
- Salt, pepper, garlic powder
- Honey
- Granola or make your own

## Meal Prep Tips

### Sunday Prep (1-2 hours)

1. **Cook brown rice in bulk** - Make 4-6 cups for the week
2. **Bake chicken thighs** - Season and bake all at once
3. **Wash and chop vegetables** - Prep carrots, broccoli, lettuce
4. **Make overnight oats** - Prepare 3-4 jars for quick breakfasts
5. **Pre-portion snacks** - Divide yogurt, granola, nuts into containers

### Daily Quick Tasks (15-20 minutes)

- Assemble meals using prepped ingredients
- Cook fresh items as needed
- Reheat pre-cooked components

---

## Budget Tips

- **Buy in bulk:** Oats, rice, and beans are much cheaper in larger quantities
- **Use what you have:** Substitute similar items (brown rice for quinoa, black beans for pinto beans)
- **Freeze extras:** Cooked chicken and rice freeze well for future meals
- **Shop sales:** Check weekly ads for protein deals
- **Generic brands:** Store brands are often identical in quality

---

## Nutrition Notes

- Each meal includes protein, whole grains, and vegetables
- Snacks combine protein/fat with carbohydrates for sustained energy
- Minimal processed foods and added sugars
- Adequate fiber from whole grains, fruits, and vegetables
- Family-friendly portions that can be adjusted based on ages

---

## Modifications

### For Younger Children (1-3 years)

- Cut food into small, safe pieces
- Reduce portion sizes to ¼-½ adult portions
- Soften vegetables well
- Avoid choking hazards (whole grapes, nuts)

### For Larger Families

- Double protein portions
- Increase produce quantities
- Consider buying family-size packages
- Batch cook in larger quantities

### For Dietary Restrictions

- **Dairy-free:** Use plant-based milk, dairy-free cheese
- **Gluten-free:** Substitute gluten-free bread, corn tortillas, rice
- **Nut allergies:** Replace peanut butter with sunflower seed butter
